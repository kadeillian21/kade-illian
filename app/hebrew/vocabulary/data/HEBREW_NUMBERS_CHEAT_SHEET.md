# Hebrew Numbers Cheat Sheet

## The Paradox: Gender Reversal く

**Most confusing rule in Hebrew numbers:**
- **Masculine** number forms are used with **feminine** nouns
- **Feminine** number forms are used with **masculine** nouns

Examples:
- 砖职止砖指 指执 (shloshah yamim) = "three (m) days" - but 止 (day) is masculine!
- 砖指止砖 砖指执 (shalosh shanim) = "three (f) years" - but 砖指指 (year) is feminine!

**Exception:** The number 1 and 2 sometimes follow normal gender agreement.

## How Hebrew Numbers Combine

### Numbers 11-19
Formed by combining the units + 注指砖指专/注侄砖职专值 (ten):
- 11 = 址址 注指砖指专 (achat asar) - literally "one ten"
- 12 = 砖职值 注指砖指专 (shneim asar) - literally "two ten"
- 15 = 植执砖指旨 注指砖指专 (chamishah asar) - literally "five ten"

### Numbers 21-99
Formed by combining tens + units (like English "twenty-three"):
- 23 = 注侄砖职专执 职砖指止砖 (esrim v'shalosh) - "twenty and three"
- 45 = 址专职指旨注执 职指值砖 (arba'im v'chamesh) - "forty and five"
- 77 = 砖执职注执 职砖执职注指 (shiv'im v'shiv'ah) - "seventy and seven"

### Hundreds
- 100 = 值指 (me'ah)
- 200 = 指转址执 (matayim) - **dual form** (special!)
- 300 = 砖职止砖 值止转 (shalosh me'ot) - "three hundreds" (plural)
- 400 = 址专职址旨注 值止转 (arba me'ot)
- 500 = 植值砖 值止转 (chamesh me'ot)

### Thousands
- 1,000 = 侄侄祝 (elef)
- 2,000 = 址职驻址旨执 (alpayim) - **dual form** (special!)
- 3,000 = 砖职止砖侄转 植指驻执 (shloshet alafim) - "three of thousands" (construct + plural)
- 5,000 = 植值砖侄转 植指驻执 (chameset alafim)

### Complex Numbers
Largest units first, connected with 职 (and):
- 365 = 砖职止砖 值止转 职砖执砖执旨 职指值砖
  - "three hundreds and sixty and five"

## Important Patterns

### The Plural Forms for Tens
Notice the pattern: Tens (20-90) are **plural forms** of the basic numbers:
- 20 = 注侄砖职专执 (esrim) - plural of 10
- 30 = 砖职止砖执 (shloshim) - plural of 3
- 40 = 址专职指旨注执 (arba'im) - plural of 4
- etc.

### Ordinals
For ordinals (first, second, third):
- Only 1-10 have special ordinal forms (专执砖止, 砖值执, 砖职执砖执...)
- Beyond 10, use cardinal numbers as adjectives
- Ordinals have masculine AND feminine forms (like adjectives)

## Theologically Significant Numbers

### 7 (砖侄址注 / 砖执职注指)
- **Most important number** in Hebrew Bible
- Sabbath, creation week, seven feasts
- Represents completion/perfection

### 40 (址专职指旨注执)
- **Most common large number**
- 40 days flood, 40 years wilderness, 40 days Moses on Sinai
- Represents testing/trial/preparation

### 12 (砖职值 注指砖指专)
- Tribes of Israel, months, apostles
- Represents God's people/governance

### 70 (砖执职注执)
- Nations of the world (Genesis 10)
- Elders of Israel
- Years of exile

### 3 (砖指止砖 / 砖职止砖指)
- Third day (resurrection pattern)
- Three patriarchs, three festivals
- Represents divine completeness

## Reading Tips for Biblical Texts

1. **Numbers are ALWAYS written as words**, never digits (no "7", only 砖侄址注)
2. **Look for the construct chain** with hundreds/thousands:
   - 砖职止砖侄转 (construct "three of") + noun
3. **Gender clues**: If you see a masculine number form, the noun is feminine!
4. **Context is key**: Ages, years, days help you know what numbers to expect

## Pro Tips

1. **Start with cardinals 1-10** - these are the foundation
2. **Learn the tens** - they're just plural forms
3. **Practice ordinals 1-7** - you'll see these constantly (creation story!)
4. **Memorize key numbers**: 7, 40, 12, 70 (theologically important)
5. **Don't stress about gender** - even native readers find it confusing at first!

## Common Biblical Phrases to Practice

- 止 侄指 (yom echad) - "day one" (Genesis 1:5)
- 止 址砖职旨执注执 (yom ha-shevi'i) - "the seventh day" (Genesis 2:2)
- 址专职指旨注执 止 (arba'im yom) - "forty days" (Genesis 7:4)
- 砖职止砖侄转 指执 (shloshet yamim) - "three days" (Exodus 3:18)
